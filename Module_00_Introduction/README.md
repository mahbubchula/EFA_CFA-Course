# Module 00: Introduction & Foundations

## Overview

This module lays the theoretical and methodological groundwork for all subsequent analyses. Before diving into factor analysis, SEM, or regression, it is essential to understand *why* these methods are needed, *how* they fit into the research process, and *what* assumptions underlie them.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:
- Explain the role of latent variables in transportation and behavioral research
- Distinguish between reflective and formative measurement models
- Describe the full analytical workflow from research design to reporting
- Identify appropriate statistical methods for different research questions

## Key Concepts

- **Latent Variable**: A variable not directly observed but inferred from observed indicators (e.g., "perceived safety" inferred from survey items)
- **Manifest/Observed Variable**: Directly measured variable (e.g., a Likert scale item)
- **Construct**: A theoretical concept operationalized through measurement items
- **Reliability**: Consistency of measurement
- **Validity**: Whether we are measuring what we intend to measure
- **Reflective Measurement**: Indicators are caused by the construct (most common in behavioral research)
- **Formative Measurement**: Indicators cause/define the construct (e.g., socioeconomic status)
